# Information Security Roles and Responsibilities Policy

## Purpose
To define and communicate clear information security roles and responsibilities, ensuring that information security activities are properly owned, resourced, and performed across the organization.

This policy establishes accountability for protecting information assets and supporting the effective operation of the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All personnel, including employees, contractors, and third parties.
- All information security roles defined within the ISMS.
- All activities related to information security governance, operations, and compliance.

## Responsibilities
- **Management**: Ensures information security roles are defined, resourced, supported, and enforced.
- **The Information Security function**: Defines information security roles and responsibilities and supports their implementation, with the support of other department managers.
- **Department Managers**: Ensure personnel within their areas understand and fulfil assigned information security responsibilities.
- **All Personnel**: Comply with assigned information security responsibilities and protect organizational information and assets.

---

## Policy Statements
- Information security roles and responsibilities must be clearly defined and communicated.
- Responsibilities must align with the Information Security Policy and all topic-specific policies.
- Accountability for information security responsibilities cannot be delegated, even if tasks are assigned to others.
- Individuals performing information security roles must be competent to perform their duties.
- The organization must provide sufficient resources to support information security roles and activities.

---

## Standards

### Definition and Allocation of Information Security Roles
The following roles are defined within the ISMS and apply where relevant to the organization:

- **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**: Provides overall direction for information security, approves ISMS strategy, and ensures alignment with business objectives.

- **Information Security Manager**: Operates and maintains the ISMS, coordinates risk management, monitoring, and audit activities, and ensures policies and controls are implemented.

- **Security Officer**: Supports day-to-day security operations and assists with incident, vulnerability, and control monitoring activities.

- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**: Oversees compliance with data protection and privacy requirements and acts as the contact point for personal data protection matters.

- **Internal Audit Department**: Independently evaluates the effectiveness of information security controls, policies, and processes and reports findings to management.

- **Employees**: Protect organizational information, comply with information security policies, and report security incidents or weaknesses.

- **Contractors**: Comply with information security requirements applicable to their engagement and protect organizational information accessed during their work.

---

### Accountability and Delegation
- Individuals assigned information security responsibilities may delegate specific tasks.
- Delegation does not remove accountability from the role holder.
- Role holders remain responsible for ensuring delegated tasks are performed correctly and effectively.

### Resource Provisioning
- The organization must determine and provide the resources required to support information security roles and responsibilities.
- Resource needs must be reviewed periodically and when significant organizational or ISMS changes occur.

### Competence and Training
- Individuals performing information security roles must have the necessary competence to perform their responsibilities effectively.
- Competence may be achieved through appropriate education, training, and experience.
- Personnel with information security responsibilities must receive **regular training**, at least **annually where feasible**, to maintain and improve their knowledge and skills.
- Training should be appropriate to the role and support continual improvement in information security performance.

---

## Procedures

### Role Assignment Procedure
1. Information security roles and responsibilities are identified.
2. Required competence for each role is defined.
3. **Human Resources (HR)** is involved in the assignment or confirmation of roles to ensure alignment with job functions, contracts, and competence requirements.
4. Roles and responsibilities are assigned to appropriate individuals.
5. Assigned roles are communicated to affected personnel.

### Role Review Procedure
1. Information security roles and responsibilities are reviewed at planned intervals.
2. Roles are updated following organizational, personnel, or ISMS changes.
3. Changes are communicated to relevant personnel.
