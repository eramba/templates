# Third-Party & Supplier Security Policy

## Purpose
To ensure that information security risks arising from third parties and suppliers are identified, assessed, contractually controlled, monitored, and managed throughout the entire lifecycle of the supplier relationship.

This policy is intended to prevent loss, misuse, unauthorized disclosure, or disruption of organizational information and systems caused by external parties.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All third parties, suppliers, contractors, service providers, and partners.
- All situations where third parties create, access, process, store, transmit, or manage organizational information or systems.
- All forms of information, including electronic, physical, and verbal.
- All stages of supplier engagement, including selection, contracting, onboarding, operation, change, and termination.

## Responsibilities
- **Management**: Ensures supplier relationships are approved, resourced, governed, and that risk acceptance and enforcement actions are authorized where required.
- **The Information Security function**: Defines supplier security requirements, reviews supplier risk and assurance evidence, monitors compliance, and manages supplier-related security incidents, **with the support of other department managers**.
- **Business / Contract Owners**: Own supplier relationships, ensure contracts include required information security clauses, and monitor supplier performance and compliance.
- **Procurement / Legal (where applicable)**: Ensures supplier agreements reflect legal, regulatory, contractual, and information security requirements.
- **Suppliers and Third Parties**: Comply with all contractual and information security requirements and implement security controls within their scope of service.
---

## Policy Statements
- Supplier relationships must be formally approved and risk assessed before engagement.
- Information security requirements must be contractually defined and documented.
- Suppliers must protect organizational information according to its classification.
- Supplier compliance must be monitored throughout the relationship.
- Information transfers to and from suppliers must be secured.
- Changes to supplier services must not introduce unmanaged security risk.
- Supplier access must be removed promptly upon termination or no longer being required.

---

## Standards

### Supplier Risk Assessment and Classification
- All suppliers must be assessed based on:
  - Information classification
  - Level of system or data access
  - Criticality of the service
  - Legal and regulatory impact
- Higher-risk suppliers must meet enhanced security and assurance requirements.

### Supplier Agreements and Security Clauses
Supplier agreements must include, where applicable:
- Description of information accessed or provided and access methods.
- Information classification and handling requirements.
- Legal, regulatory, and contractual compliance obligations.
- Defined security controls, including access control, monitoring, and logging.
- Acceptable and unacceptable use of information and assets.
- Authorization and removal of supplier personnel access.
- Incident notification and collaboration requirements.
- Sub-contracting restrictions and approval requirements.
- Right to audit and assurance requirements.
- Reporting obligations and remediation timelines.
- Business continuity, backup, and disaster recovery requirements.
- Change management requirements, including advance notification.
- Termination, exit, and secure disposal obligations.

### Supplier Information Transfer Security
- Information transfers must reflect the classification of the information.
- Transfer methods must be approved and documented.
- Controls must protect against interception, unauthorized access, loss, or alteration.
- Responsibilities and liabilities for transfer-related incidents must be defined.

#### Electronic Transfer
- Sensitive information must be protected (e.g. encryption).
- Malware protection must be implemented.
- Strong authentication must be used over public networks.
- Use of external public services (e.g. file sharing, messaging) requires approval.
- Automatic forwarding to external addresses must be restricted.
- SMS, instant messaging, and fax must not be used for sensitive information unless approved.

#### Physical Media Transfer
- Only approved couriers may be used.
- Packaging must protect against damage and unauthorized access.
- Tamper-evident controls must be used where required.
- Transfers must be logged, including sender, recipient, and receipt confirmation.

#### Verbal Information Transfer
- Confidential discussions must not occur in public or insecure environments.
- Participants must be authorized.
- Appropriate room and environmental controls must be used.
- Sensitive conversations must start with a classification disclaimer.

### ICT Supply Chain Security
- Security requirements must be defined before acquiring ICT products or services.
- Suppliers must propagate security requirements to sub-suppliers.
- Suppliers must provide:
  - Information on software and hardware components
  - Security functions and secure configuration guidance
- Delivered products and services must be validated for compliance.
- Critical components must be identifiable, traceable, and assured.
- Measures must ensure components are genuine and untampered.
- ICT lifecycle and availability risks must be actively managed.

### Monitoring, Review, and Change Management
- Supplier performance and security compliance must be monitored.
- Changes to services, technology, locations, or sub-suppliers must be reviewed.
- Supplier security incidents must be reported, investigated, and remediated.
- Regular reviews, audits, or assurance activities must be conducted.
- Deficiencies must result in corrective actions, escalation, or termination if unresolved.

## Procedures

### Supplier Onboarding Procedure
1. Supplier need is identified and approved.
2. Supplier risk assessment is performed.
3. Required security controls and clauses are defined.
4. Contract is reviewed and approved.
5. Supplier access is provisioned.

### Supplier Monitoring and Review Procedure
1. Supplier performance and security evidence are reviewed periodically.
2. Changes and incidents are assessed for impact.
3. Issues are tracked and remediated.
4. Risk level is updated as required.

### Supplier Termination Procedure
1. Supplier access is revoked.
2. Organizational information and assets are returned or securely destroyed.
3. Confirmation of disposal is obtained where required.
4. Supplier records are updated and archived.
