# Human Resources Security

## Purpose
To ensure that security responsibilities and controls related to personnel are applied throughout the employment or contractual lifecycle, reducing risks arising from human factors before, during, and after engagement with the organization.

## Scope
This document applies to all employees, contractors, temporary staff, and other personnel with access to organizational assets, systems, or information.

## Responsibilities
- **Human Resources** is responsible for managing personnel processes in accordance with this policy.
- **Department Managers** are responsible for defining roles, supervising personnel, and ensuring security responsibilities are understood.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining human resources security requirements and supporting their implementation, with the assistance of Department Managers.
- **Employees and Contractors** are responsible for complying with security policies and assigned responsibilities.

## Policy Statements
- Security responsibilities must be defined and applied throughout the personnel lifecycle.
- Personnel must be suitable for the roles they are assigned.
- Personnel must understand and comply with organizational security requirements.
- Access to assets and information must be aligned with employment or contractual status.
- Security obligations may continue after termination where applicable.
- Violations of security requirements may result in disciplinary action.

## Standards

### Screening
- Background checks must be performed where legally permitted and appropriate to the role.
- Screening requirements must align with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

### Contracts, Confidentiality, and NDAs
- Employment and contractor agreements must define security responsibilities.
- Confidentiality obligations must be included in contracts.
- Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) must be in place where access to sensitive or restricted information is required.
- Confidentiality obligations must remain in effect after termination where applicable.

### Onboarding
- Roles and responsibilities must be clearly defined prior to granting access.
- Access must be provisioned in accordance with approved organizational roles.

### During Employment
- Security responsibilities must be communicated and acknowledged.
- Changes to roles or responsibilities must be reflected in access rights.
- Personnel must comply with all organizational security policies and standards.

### Remote Working
- Remote working arrangements must be approved by management.
- Personnel working remotely must comply with organizational security requirements.
- Organizational data and systems accessed remotely must be protected in accordance with applicable policies.

### Termination and Exit
- **Standard Exit**
  - Access to systems and assets must be revoked upon termination or contract end.
  - Organizational assets must be returned.
- **Emergency Exit**
  - In cases of immediate risk, access may be revoked without prior notice.
  - Emergency access removal must be documented and reviewed after the fact.
- Confidentiality and security obligations must be reinforced upon exit.

### Disciplinary Process
- Violations of security policies may result in disciplinary action.
- Disciplinary measures must follow standard organizational procedures and applicable laws.
- Security incidents involving personnel may trigger disciplinary review.

## Procedures

### Onboarding Procedure
1. Employment or contract terms are established.
2. Screening requirements are completed where applicable.
3. Security responsibilities and confidentiality obligations are communicated.
4. Access is provisioned based on approved roles.

### Role Change Procedure
1. Role changes are approved and documented.
2. Access rights are reviewed and adjusted accordingly.
3. Changes are logged where required.

### Offboarding Procedure
1. Termination or contract end is confirmed.
2. Standard or emergency access revocation is performed as required.
3. Assets are returned.
4. Ongoing confidentiality obligations are communicated.
