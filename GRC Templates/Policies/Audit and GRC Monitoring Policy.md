## Standards

### Monitoring and Measurement
- Information security performance must be monitored using defined metrics mapped to:
  - Controls
  - Risks
  - Policies
  - Key ISMS processes
- Monitoring activities must be repeatable and produce comparable results over time.
- Monitoring results must be documented and retained as evidence.
- Monitoring outcomes must be reviewed by the Information Security function, with the support of other department managers.

---

### Internal Audit Programme
- An internal audit programme must be established and maintained.
- Internal audits shall be performed at **planned and risk-based intervals**.
- Internal audits shall ensure that:
  - All internal controls are audited over time.
  - Controls are implemented and operating as intended.
  - Controls adhere to approved **policies, standards, and procedures**.
- Control owners shall:
  - Be informed of audit results.
  - Receive clear feedback on deficiencies and required improvements.
  - Be responsible for implementing corrective actions.
- Audit results and control testing performance shall be:
  - Tracked until closure.
  - Reported at least **annually** to management during the management committee review.
- Audit scope, results, and evidence must be documented and retained.

---

### Independent Review of Information Security
- An independent review of information security shall be conducted **at least every two years**.
- Independent reviews must:
  - Be performed by a qualified **third party**.
  - Cover the **entire ISMS**, including policies, risks, controls, and processes.
- The objectives of the review are to:
  - Assess the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS.
  - Identify gaps, weaknesses, and improvement opportunities.
- Results of independent reviews must be:
  - Documented.
  - Reported to management.
- Identified findings must result in corrective actions that are tracked to completion.

---

### Risk and Policy Review
- Information security risks must be reviewed at least **annually**.
- Risk reviews shall:
  - Confirm continued relevance.
  - Reassess likelihood and impact.
  - Validate risk treatment effectiveness.
- Information security policies must be reviewed at least **annually** to ensure they remain appropriate, effective, and aligned with business and regulatory requirements.
- Reviews and approvals must be documented.

---

### Management Review
- Management shall review the ISMS at planned intervals.
- The annual management committee review must include:
  - Overall control testing and audit performance.
  - Results of internal audits and independent reviews.
  - Risk status and treatment effectiveness.
  - Policy review status.
  - Opportunities for continual improvement.
- Decisions and actions resulting from management reviews must be documented and tracked.
