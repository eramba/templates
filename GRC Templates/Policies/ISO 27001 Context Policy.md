# ISO 27001 Context Policy

## Purpose
To define the organizational context, interested parties, ISMS scope, information security policy framework, and information security objectives, ensuring that the Information Security Management System (ISMS) is aligned with the organization’s purpose, strategy, and obligations.

This policy establishes the foundation for the design, implementation, operation, and continual improvement of the ISMS.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- Eramba Limited as an organization.
- The Information Security Management System (ISMS).
- All information assets, processes, systems, and services within the defined ISMS scope.
- All personnel involved in information security governance and decision-making.

## Responsibilities
- **Top Management**: Defines organizational direction, approves the ISMS scope, policy, and objectives, and ensures alignment with business strategy.
- **The Information Security function**: Facilitates context analysis, maintains the ISMS scope, and supports the definition and monitoring of information security objectives, with the support of other department managers.
- **Department Managers**: Provide input on internal and external issues, interested parties, and objectives relevant to their areas.
- **All Personnel**: Comply with the Information Security Policy and support the achievement of information security objectives.

---

## Policy Statements
- Internal and external issues relevant to information security shall be identified and reviewed.
- Interested parties and their information security requirements shall be determined.
- The scope of the ISMS shall be clearly defined, documented, and maintained.
- An Information Security Policy shall be established and maintained.
- Information security objectives shall be defined, monitored, and reviewed.
- The ISMS shall be continually improved.

---

## Standards

### Organizational Context
The organization identifies and maintains an understanding of internal and external issues that are relevant to its purpose and that affect the effectiveness of the ISMS.

| Type     | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Internal | Eramba Limited develops and operates a SaaS GRC platform that processes customer data and configuration information, making confidentiality, integrity, and availability critical. |
| Internal | The organization operates with a fully remote workforce, increasing reliance on cloud services, identity management, and secure remote access. |
| Internal | Product development, customer support, and operations require controlled access to shared systems and environments. |
| External | Customers operate in regulated environments and expect alignment with ISO 27001 and similar information security standards. |
| External | The organization relies on third-party cloud and SaaS providers for hosting, identity, communications, and monitoring services. |
| External | The threat landscape includes attacks targeting SaaS providers, including data breaches, ransomware, and supply-chain compromises. |

Organizational context is reviewed periodically and when significant business, technological, regulatory, or threat-related changes occur.

---

### Interested Parties and Requirements
The organization identifies interested parties relevant to the ISMS and determines their information security requirements.

| Interested Party | Relevant Requirements | Addressed by the ISMS |
|------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Customers | Protection of customer data, service availability, confidentiality, integrity, contractual security commitments, incident notification | Yes |
| Employees | Secure access to systems, protection of personal data, clear security responsibilities | Yes |
| Contractors | Controlled access, confidentiality obligations, secure handling of information | Yes |
| Regulators / Authorities | Compliance with applicable data protection and information security regulations | Yes |
| Suppliers and Cloud Providers | Defined security requirements, incident notification, continuity obligations | Yes |
| Owners / Shareholders | Protection of organizational assets, business continuity, reputation, and compliance | Yes |

Interested party requirements are reviewed periodically and when contractual, regulatory, or business changes occur.

---

### ISMS Scope
The Information Security Management System (ISMS) applies to **Eramba Limited** and covers the **design, development, operation, hosting, and support** of the Eramba software platform provided to customers as a **Software-as-a-Service (SaaS)** solution.

The scope includes:
- The Eramba GRC software application developed and maintained by Eramba Limited.
- Cloud infrastructure, platforms, and services used to host and operate the Eramba SaaS platform.
- Supporting systems and tools used for development, deployment, monitoring, support, and maintenance.
- Information assets processed, stored, or transmitted by the Eramba platform, including customer data.
- Business processes supporting the SaaS service, including product development, operations, customer support, sales, and internal administration.
- Employees and contractors who access or manage systems and information within scope.
- Interfaces and dependencies with third-party service providers supporting the delivery and operation of the Eramba SaaS platform.

The ISMS does not include customer-managed environments or systems not operated or controlled by Eramba Limited, except where explicitly defined through contractual or operational interfaces.

The ISMS scope is reviewed periodically and whenever significant organizational, technical, or regulatory changes occur.

---

### Information Security Policy Framework
The organization maintains an Information Security Policy that:
- Is appropriate to the organization’s purpose and context.
- Provides the framework for setting information security objectives.
- Commits to satisfying applicable information security requirements.
- Commits to continual improvement of the ISMS.

The Information Security Policy is:
- Documented
- Communicated internally
- Made available to interested parties where appropriate

Topic-specific policies support and align with the Information Security Policy.

---

### Information Security Objectives
The organization establishes the following information security objectives:

- **Protect customer data** by preventing unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of information processed by the Eramba SaaS platform.
- **Maintain availability of the Eramba platform** by minimizing service disruptions and meeting defined recovery objectives for critical systems.
- **Manage information security risks effectively** by performing regular risk assessments and ensuring risks are treated in line with the Risk Management Policy.
- **Maintain customer and regulatory trust** by complying with applicable information security requirements and maintaining alignment with ISO 27001.

Information security objectives are documented, communicated, monitored, and reviewed as appropriate.

---

### Planning to Achieve Information Security Objectives
For each information security objective, the organization determines:
- What actions will be taken
- What resources are required
- Who is responsible
- Target completion timelines
- How results will be evaluated

Progress against objectives is reviewed periodically and feeds into management review and continual improvement activities.

---

## Procedures

### Context and Interested Party Review Procedure
1. Identify internal and external issues affecting information security.
2. Identify relevant interested parties.
3. Identify applicable information security requirements.
4. Review and update context and requirements as needed.

### ISMS Scope Review Procedure
1. Review organizational context and dependencies.
2. Confirm ISMS boundaries and applicability.
3. Update scope documentation if required.
4. Obtain management approval.

### Information Security Objectives Procedure
1. Define information security objectives.
2. Align objectives with risks and policy.
3. Assign responsibilities and resources.
4. Monitor progress and effectiveness.
5. Review and update objectives.
