# Operations Management Policy

## Purpose
To ensure that operational changes are controlled and that system capacity is planned and managed effectively, reducing the risk of service disruption, performance degradation, or security incidents.

This policy supports the stable and secure operation of organizational systems and services.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All production and non-production systems, applications, infrastructure, and services.
- All changes that may impact availability, performance, security, or integrity of systems.
- All capacity-related activities affecting systems, networks, and services.
- All personnel involved in operational management, system administration, and service delivery.

## Responsibilities
- **Management**: Ensures operational management activities are governed, resourced, and aligned with business needs.
- **The Information Security function**: Defines security requirements related to operational changes and capacity planning, with the support of other department managers.
- **IT / Operations Teams**: Plan, implement, and manage changes and capacity in accordance with this policy.
- **System and Service Owners**: Approve changes affecting their systems and ensure capacity meets business requirements.
- **Suppliers and Service Providers**: Comply with operational management requirements defined in contracts and agreements.

---

## Policy Statements
- Operational changes must be planned, assessed, approved, and documented.
- Changes must not introduce unmanaged security, availability, or performance risks.
- System and service capacity must be monitored and managed proactively.
- Capacity limitations must be identified and addressed before they impact operations.
- Operational management activities must be repeatable, controlled, and auditable.

---

## Standards

### Change Management
- All changes to systems, applications, infrastructure, and configurations must follow a defined change management process.
- Changes must be:
  - Classified (e.g. standard, normal, emergency).
  - Assessed for impact on availability, performance, and security.
  - Approved before implementation.
- Change implementation must:
  - Be planned and scheduled.
  - Include rollback or recovery considerations.
  - Avoid unnecessary disruption to business operations.
- Emergency changes must:
  - Be justified and documented.
  - Be reviewed retrospectively.
- Changes must be tested where feasible prior to deployment.
- Change records must be maintained.

### Capacity Management
- Capacity requirements must be identified for systems and services.
- Capacity management must consider:
  - Current utilization.
  - Expected growth.
  - Business and operational requirements.
- Capacity must be monitored to identify trends and potential constraints.
- Capacity thresholds and alerts should be defined where technically feasible.
- Capacity-related risks must be addressed before service levels are impacted.
- Capacity planning must be reviewed periodically and when significant changes occur.

---

## Procedures

### Change Management Procedure
1. Change request is raised and documented.
2. Change is assessed for impact and risk.
3. Required approvals are obtained.
4. Change is implemented according to plan.
5. Change outcome is reviewed and documented.

### Capacity Management Procedure
1. Identify capacity requirements for systems and services.
2. Monitor utilization and performance.
3. Analyse trends and forecast future needs.
4. Plan and implement capacity adjustments.
5. Review effectiveness and update plans as required.
