# Asset Management

## Purpose
To ensure that organizational assets are identified, tracked, and maintained throughout their lifecycle to support business operations, security, and compliance requirements.

## Scope
This document applies to all key information assets owned, leased, or managed by the organization, including hardware, software, cloud resources, data repositories, and supporting infrastructure.

## Responsibilities
- **Department Managers** are responsible for identifying assets used within their areas, approving asset usage by employees and contractors, and ensuring inventories are maintained.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining asset management requirements and supporting departments in their application.
- **Users (employees and contractors)** are responsible for using organizational assets in accordance with policies and standards and returning assets when required.

## Policy Statements
- Key organizational assets must be identified and inventoried.
- Assets must have an assigned owner.
- Asset inventories may be managed independently by each department, provided they meet organizational requirements.
- Assets must be used only for authorized business purposes.
- Assets must be protected against unauthorized access, loss, damage, or misuse.
- Assets provided to employees or contractors must be returned upon termination of employment, end of contract, or when no longer required.
- Employees and contractors may use personally owned devices for organizational purposes only if explicitly approved by their department manager; otherwise, personal devices must not be used.
- Asset management practices must support legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

## Standards

### Asset Inventory
- An asset inventory must exist for all key assets.
- Asset inventories must be accurate and kept up to date.
- Asset inventories may be maintained at the department level.
- Asset inventories must identify, at a minimum:
  - Asset name or identifier
  - Asset type (e.g. hardware, software, cloud service, data system)
  - Asset owner
- Asset inventories must be reviewed periodically.

### Asset Lifecycle Management
- Assets must be managed throughout their lifecycle, including acquisition, use, maintenance, and disposal.
- Assets must be approved prior to acquisition.
- Changes to assets must follow standard organizational processes.
- Assets must be securely disposed of at end of life.
- Asset assignment and return must be tracked where assets are issued to employees or contractors.

### Asset Protection
- Assets must be protected using security controls appropriate to their use.
- Protection measures must include physical, technical, and administrative controls.
- Access to assets must be restricted based on business need and least privilege.

## Procedures

### Asset Inventory Procedure
1. Departments identify key assets within their scope.
2. Assets are recorded in the relevant department asset inventory.
3. Asset owners are assigned.
4. Required asset attributes are documented.
5. Inventories are reviewed and updated periodically.

### Asset Lifecycle Procedure
1. Assets are approved prior to acquisition.
2. Assets are configured and deployed securely.
3. Assets are assigned to employees or contractors where applicable.
4. Assets are maintained and monitored.
5. Assets are returned, decommissioned, and disposed of securely when no longer required.
