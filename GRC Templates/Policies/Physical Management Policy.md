# Physical Security

## Purpose
To protect organizational personnel, assets, systems, and information from unauthorized physical access, damage, or interference.

## Scope
This document applies to all physical locations operated or used by the organization, including offices, data centers, server rooms, storage areas, and any location where organizational assets or information are stored or processed.

## Responsibilities
- **Department Managers** are responsible for ensuring physical security requirements are followed within their areas.
- **Facilities or Office Management** (where applicable) is responsible for implementing and maintaining physical security controls.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining physical security requirements and supporting their implementation, with the assistance of Department Managers.
- **Users (employees and contractors)** are responsible for complying with physical security controls and reporting issues or incidents.

## Policy Statements
- Physical access to organizational facilities must be controlled and restricted based on business need.
- Physical security controls must protect assets from unauthorized access, theft, damage, or disruption.
- Sensitive areas must be clearly identified and protected with additional controls.
- Physical security practices must support legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

## Standards

### Physical Access Control
- Access to organizational facilities must be restricted to authorized individuals.
- Physical access controls may include keys, badges, access cards, biometric controls, or equivalent mechanisms.
- Security guards may be deployed at entrances where appropriate.
- Access rights must be granted based on business need and reviewed periodically.
- Visitors must be identified, authorized, and escorted where required.

### Monitoring and Guarding
- Physical security monitoring controls must be implemented where appropriate.
- Security guards may perform regular patrols or rounds to monitor facilities and detect suspicious activity.
- Guards may check offices and common areas to ensure doors, windows, and secure areas are properly closed.
- Guards may monitor entrances and exits to deter unauthorized removal of assets.
- When third parties or suppliers perform on-site work, their activities must be supervised as appropriate.
- Suppliers and contractors may be checked upon exit to reduce the risk of unauthorized removal of organizational assets, in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

### Secure Areas
- Areas containing sensitive systems, data, or infrastructure must be designated as secure areas.
- Secure areas must be protected by appropriate physical controls, such as controlled entry points and monitoring.
- Access to secure areas must be limited to authorized personnel only.

### Monitoring and Surveillance
- Physical security monitoring controls must be implemented where appropriate.
- Monitoring controls may include motion sensors, alarms, and intrusion detection mechanisms.
- Closed-circuit television (CCTV) systems may be used to monitor entrances, exits, and sensitive areas.
- CCTV recordings must be protected from unauthorized access, modification, or deletion.
- Retention of CCTV recordings must comply with legal, regulatory, and privacy requirements.

### Facility and Equipment Maintenance
- Facilities and physical security controls must be maintained to ensure continued effectiveness.
- Maintenance activities must be planned and performed in a controlled manner.
- Only authorized and qualified personnel or approved third parties may perform maintenance on facilities, security controls, or critical equipment.
- Maintenance activities that could impact security controls must be reviewed and coordinated in advance.
- Physical security controls and facilities must be reviewed periodically to identify weaknesses or required improvements.

### Protection of Equipment
- Equipment must be protected against physical threats such as theft, fire, water damage, and environmental hazards.
- Critical equipment must be located in areas with appropriate environmental controls where required.
- Cabling and network infrastructure must be protected against unauthorized access or tampering.

### Clear Desk and Clear Screen
- Sensitive information must not be left unattended in unsecured areas.
- Workstations must be locked when unattended.
- Printed sensitive information must be secured or disposed of properly when not in use.

## Procedures

### Visitor Management Procedure
1. Visitors are identified and registered upon arrival.
2. Visitor access is approved and limited to required areas.
3. Visitors are escorted where necessary.
4. Visitor access is revoked upon departure.

### Physical Access Review Procedure
1. Physical access rights are reviewed periodically.
2. Access for individuals no longer requiring entry is removed promptly.
3. Reviews are documented where required.
