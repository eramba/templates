# Information Security Incident Management

## Purpose
To ensure that information security incidents are identified, reported, assessed, managed, and resolved in a consistent and effective manner, minimizing impact to the organization and supporting legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

## Scope
This document applies to all information security incidents affecting organizational systems, data, services, or assets, including incidents involving employees, contractors, third parties, or external parties.

## Responsibilities
- **All Users** are responsible for promptly reporting suspected or actual information security incidents.
- **Department Managers** are responsible for supporting incident response activities within their areas.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining incident management requirements and coordinating incident response, with the assistance of Department Managers.
- **IT / Technical Teams** are responsible for technical investigation, containment, and remediation activities where applicable.
- **Legal, Compliance, and Management** are involved where required based on incident severity.

## Policy Statements
- Information security incidents must be reported and managed in a timely and structured manner.
- Incident response activities must prioritize safety, containment, and impact reduction.
- Evidence related to incidents must be preserved where required.
- Lessons learned from incidents must be used to improve security controls.
- Incident management practices must support legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.
- Personnel must be able to report security concerns without fear of retaliation.

## Standards

### Incident Identification and Reporting
- Information security **events** and **incidents** must be identified and reported as soon as they are detected or suspected.
- Reporting channels must be clearly defined and communicated.
- Incidents and concerns may include, but are not limited to:
  - Malware alerts
  - Suspicious or failed authentication activity
  - Unauthorized access or data disclosure
  - Loss or theft of devices
  - System compromise or abnormal behavior
  - Policy violations with security impact

### Whistleblowing and Anonymous Reporting
- The organization must provide a mechanism for confidential or anonymous reporting of security concerns.
- Personnel may report suspected incidents, policy violations, or unethical behavior without identifying themselves.
- An anonymous reporting channel must be maintained (e.g. **anonymous_incident@company.com**).
- Reports submitted through anonymous channels must be reviewed and handled with the same level of seriousness as identified reports.
- Retaliation against individuals who report concerns in good faith is prohibited.

### Incident Classification and Prioritization
- An **information security event** is any observable occurrence in a system, network, or service that may be relevant to security.
- An **information security incident** is one or more events that have been assessed and determined to pose a real or potential threat to the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of organizational assets.
- All reported events must be reviewed to determine whether they constitute an incident.
- Events that do not pose a security risk must be recorded and closed.
- Events that indicate compromise, policy violation, or material risk must be escalated and classified as incidents.
- Incidents must be classified and prioritized based on potential and actual impact, including:
  - Impact on confidentiality, integrity, and availability
  - Scope of affected systems, data, or users
  - Legal, regulatory, or contractual implications
  - **Potential financial, operational, and reputational cost to the organization**
  - Urgency and likelihood of further impact

### Incident Response and Handling
- Incident response must follow a defined and repeatable process.
- Response activities may include:
  - Containment and mitigation
  - Investigation and analysis
  - Eradication of root causes
  - Recovery of systems and services
- Actions taken must be documented.

### Communication and Escalation
- Incidents must be escalated to management based on severity.
- Internal and external communications must be controlled and coordinated.
- Notifications to customers, partners, or authorities must be approved and managed where required.

### Evidence Handling
- Evidence related to incidents must be preserved to support investigation or legal action.
- Access to evidence must be restricted.
- Chain-of-custody must be maintained where applicable.

### Post-Incident Review
- Significant incidents must be reviewed after resolution.
- Root causes and contributing factors must be identified.
- Corrective actions must be defined and tracked.

## Procedures

### Incident Response Procedure
1. Event or incident is detected or reported (including anonymous reports).
2. Event is logged and reviewed.
3. Determination is made whether the event constitutes an incident.
4. Incidents are classified and prioritized.
5. Containment and mitigation actions are performed.
6. Investigation and remediation are completed.
7. Systems and services are recovered.
8. Incident is closed and documented.

### Incident Review Procedure
1. Incident details are reviewed.
2. Lessons learned are identified.
3. Improvements to controls or processes are defined.
4. Actions are tracked to completion.
