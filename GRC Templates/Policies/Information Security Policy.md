# Information Security Policy

## Purpose
To define the organization’s approach to managing information security, establish guiding principles, and set management direction for protecting information and information systems.

This policy provides the framework for setting information security objectives and for governing information security across the organization.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All information assets owned, processed, or managed by the organization.
- All systems, services, and processes within the ISMS scope.
- All personnel, including employees, contractors, and third parties with access to organizational information or systems.

## Responsibilities
- **Top Management**: Approves this policy and demonstrates commitment to information security.
- **The Information Security function**: Maintains this policy and supports its implementation, with the support of other department managers.
- **Department Managers**: Ensure this policy is implemented within their areas.
- **All Personnel**: Understand, acknowledge, and comply with this policy and supporting information security policies.

---

## Policy Statements

### Definition of Information Security
Information security is the preservation of the **confidentiality, integrity, and availability** of information, regardless of its form.

### Information Security Objectives
The organization establishes information security objectives to:
- Protect information assets against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or loss.
- Maintain availability of systems and services supporting critical business processes.
- Manage information security risks in line with the Risk Management Policy.
- Maintain customer, regulatory, and stakeholder trust.

### Information Security Principles
All information security activities shall be guided by the following principles:
- Information security supports business objectives.
- Risk-based decision making is applied.
- Least privilege and need-to-know access is enforced.
- Security controls are proportionate to risk.
- Information security is a shared responsibility.

### Compliance and Applicable Requirements
The organization commits to satisfying applicable:
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Contractual obligations
- Information security requirements agreed with customers and suppliers

### Continual Improvement
The organization commits to the continual improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS) through:
- Risk assessment and treatment
- Monitoring and measurement
- Audits and reviews
- Management oversight

### Roles and Responsibilities
Information security roles and responsibilities are defined and governed through the **Information Security Roles and Responsibilities Policy**.

### Exceptions and Exemptions
- Exceptions to this policy or supporting information security policies must be formally requested.
- Exceptions must be risk-assessed, approved, documented, and time-bound.
- Approved exceptions must be reviewed periodically.

---

## Standards

### Approval, Communication, and Awareness
- This policy shall be approved by **Top Management**.
- The policy shall be:
  - Documented
  - Communicated to relevant personnel
  - Made available to interested parties where appropriate
- Personnel shall be required to acknowledge understanding and compliance where applicable.

### Review and Update
- This policy shall be reviewed:
  - At planned intervals
  - When significant changes occur to business strategy, technology, regulations, risks, or the threat environment
- Reviews shall consider:
  - Audit results
  - Management review outputs
  - Lessons learned from information security incidents
- Changes to this policy require **Top Management approval**.
