# Acceptable Use of Assets

## Purpose
To define acceptable and prohibited use of organizational assets in order to protect information, systems, and resources from misuse, damage, or unauthorized activity.

## Scope
This document applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who access or use organizational assets, including hardware, software, systems, networks, and data, whether owned by the organization or approved for organizational use.

## Responsibilities
- **Users (employees and contractors)** are responsible for using organizational assets in accordance with this policy and related standards.
- **Department Managers** are responsible for ensuring users within their areas understand and comply with acceptable use requirements.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining acceptable use requirements and supporting enforcement where necessary, **with the support of Department Managers**.

## Policy Statements
- Organizational assets must be used primarily for legitimate business purposes.
- Limited personal use of organizational assets is permitted only if it does not interfere with business operations, security, or compliance requirements.
- Users must comply with all security policies, standards, and procedures when using organizational assets.
- Users must not engage in activities that could expose the organization to legal, financial, or reputational risk.
- Users are responsible for protecting assets assigned to them from loss, theft, damage, or misuse.

## Standards

### Acceptable Use
- Assets may be used only by authorized users.
- Access credentials must not be shared.
- Software and services used must be approved by the organization.
- Organizational data must be handled in accordance with the Data Management policy.
- Internet and network access must be used responsibly and lawfully.

### Prohibited Use
- Unauthorized access to systems, data, or networks.
- Circumventing or disabling security controls.
- Installing unauthorized software or hardware.
- Using assets for illegal activities or activities that violate organizational policies.
- Accessing, creating, or distributing offensive, inappropriate, or unlawful content.
- Using organizational assets to intentionally disrupt operations or security.

### Personal Devices
- Personally owned devices may be used for organizational purposes only if explicitly approved by the user’s department manager.
- Approved personal devices must comply with applicable security requirements.
- Unapproved personal devices must not be used to access organizational systems or data.

### Asset Protection
- Users must take reasonable steps to protect assets from loss, theft, or damage.
- Suspected loss, theft, or compromise of assets must be reported promptly through established channels.
- Assets must be returned upon termination of employment, end of contract, or when no longer required.

## Procedures

### User Acknowledgement
1. Users are informed of acceptable use requirements.
2. Users acknowledge their responsibilities prior to being granted access to organizational assets.

### Asset Return Procedure
1. Assets assigned to users are tracked.
2. Assets are returned upon termination, contract completion, or reassignment.
3. Returned assets are reviewed and processed according to organizational procedures.
