# Security Awareness

## Purpose
To ensure that all personnel understand their information security responsibilities and are aware of common security risks, threats, and expected behaviours in order to reduce the likelihood of security incidents.

## Scope
This document applies to all employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third parties who have access to the organization’s information, systems, or facilities.

## Responsibilities
- Management is responsible for supporting and enforcing security awareness activities.
- The Information Security function is responsible for defining and maintaining security awareness content, with support from relevant departments where required.
- All personnel are responsible for complying with security awareness requirements and applying them in their daily activities.

## Policy Statements
- All personnel must participate in regular security awareness training.
- Security awareness training must be appropriate to the individual’s role and responsibilities.
- Personnel must understand and comply with the organization’s information security policies and standards.
- Failure to follow security awareness requirements may result in disciplinary action.

## Standards
- Security awareness training must be provided to all personnel at least annually.
- Security awareness training must be provided as part of onboarding for new personnel.
- Security awareness content must cover, at a minimum:
  - Information security responsibilities
  - Acceptable use of information and systems
  - Password and authentication security
  - Phishing and social engineering risks
  - Malware and ransomware risks
  - Data protection and confidentiality
- Role-specific security awareness must be provided where personnel have elevated access or responsibilities.
- Completion of security awareness activities must be tracked and recorded.
- Security awareness materials must be reviewed and updated periodically to reflect current threats and risks.

## Procedures

### Security Awareness Training Procedure
1. Security awareness training content is defined and maintained by the Information Security function.
2. Training is delivered to all personnel through organization-approved methods.
3. New personnel complete security awareness training as part of onboarding.
4. Existing personnel complete security awareness training at least annually.
5. Completion records are collected and retained as evidence.

### Ongoing Awareness Activities Procedure
1. Periodic security awareness communications are distributed to personnel.
2. Awareness activities may include newsletters, alerts, campaigns, or simulations.
3. Topics are selected based on current threats, incidents, or risk assessments.
4. Participation and effectiveness may be monitored where applicable.
