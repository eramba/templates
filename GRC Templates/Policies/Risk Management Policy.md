# Risk Management Policy

## Purpose
To define a consistent and repeatable approach for identifying, analysing, evaluating, treating, and monitoring information security risks, ensuring that risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information are understood and managed within acceptable levels.

## Scope
This policy applies to all information within the scope of the ISMS, including business processes, systems, assets, services, and third-party relationships.

## Responsibilities
- **Management**: Ensures the risk management process is established, resourced, and aligned with business objectives.
- **The Information Security function**: Defines and maintains the risk assessment and treatment methodology and ensures consistency of application, with the support of other department managers.
- **Risk Owners**: Are accountable for risks, approve treatment actions within their authority, and accept residual risks.
- **Department Managers**: Support risk assessment and treatment activities.
- **All Personnel**: Contribute to identifying and reporting risks.

---

## Policy Statements
- Information security risks must be assessed using defined likelihood and impact criteria.
- Risk levels must be derived using a likelihood × impact matrix.
- Risks must be treated according to their classified risk level.
- Risk treatment and acceptance must follow defined approval requirements.
- All risk decisions must be documented and auditable.

---

## Standards

### Risk Classification Criteria

#### Likelihood
- **High (3)**: Happened before and could happen again.
- **Medium (2)**: Never happened before but could happen.
- **Low (1)**: Never happened before and is unlikely to happen.

#### Impact
- **High (3)**: Contractual or legal repercussions.
- **Medium (2)**: Reputational repercussions.
- **Low (1)**: Negligible impact to customers or the organization.

#### Risk Level Determination
- Risk level is calculated as **Likelihood × Impact**.
- The resulting risk score is mapped to a risk level using the approved risk matrix.

---

### Risk Treatment Matrix and Approval Requirements

Risk treatment decisions must follow the risk matrix defined by likelihood and impact, with associated approval requirements:

#### Low Risk
- Characteristics:
  - Low overall impact to the organization.
  - Risk is considered acceptable.
- Treatment:
  - Risk may be accepted or treated at the discretion of the risk owner.
- Approval:
  - **Risk Owner approval only**.
- No additional management approval is required.

#### Medium Risk
- Characteristics:
  - Noticeable business or reputational impact.
  - Risk requires active management.
- Treatment:
  - Risk must be treated using internal controls, policies, exceptions, or projects.
- Approval:
  - **Risk Owner**
  - **Risk Owner’s Manager**
  - **GRC Manager**

#### High (Serious) Risk
- Characteristics:
  - Significant contractual, legal, or business impact.
  - Risk is not acceptable without executive awareness.
- Treatment:
  - Risk must be treated and tracked until reduced to an acceptable level or formally accepted.
- Approval:
  - **Risk Owner**
  - **Risk Owner’s Manager**
  - **GRC Manager**
  - **Director-level approval**

---

### Information Security Risk Assessment
- Risks must be identified for loss of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
- Each risk must:
  - Have an assigned risk owner
  - Be analysed for likelihood and impact
  - Be classified using the risk matrix
- Risks must be prioritised based on their risk level.

---

### Information Security Risk Treatment
- Risks exceeding acceptance thresholds must be treated using one or more of the following **Eramba-aligned mechanisms**:
  - **Internal Controls**
  - **Policies**
  - **Exceptions**
  - **Projects**
- Residual risk must be reassessed after treatment.
- Risk acceptance must follow the approval requirements defined in the risk matrix.

---

### Risk Assessment Frequency and Triggers
- Risk assessments must be performed:
  - At planned intervals
  - When significant changes occur, including:
    - New systems or services
    - Material changes to existing systems
    - Security incidents
    - Supplier or regulatory changes

---

### Documentation and Records
- The organization shall retain documented information for:
  - Risk assessment results
  - Risk classification and treatment decisions
  - Risk approvals and residual risk acceptance
- Records must be complete, current, and auditable.

---

## Procedures

### Information Security Risk Assessment Procedure
1. Identify assets and risks.
2. Assign a risk owner.
3. Assess likelihood and impact.
4. Classify the risk using the risk matrix.
5. Document results.

### Information Security Risk Treatment Procedure
1. Identify risks requiring treatment.
2. Select the appropriate treatment mechanism:
   - Internal control
   - Policy
   - Exception
   - Project
3. Obtain approvals according to the risk level.
4. Implement treatment actions.
5. Reassess and document residual risk.

### Risk Review Procedure
1. Review risks at planned intervals.
2. Reassess risks after significant changes.
3. Update risk ratings, treatments, and approvals.
4. Report risk status to management.
