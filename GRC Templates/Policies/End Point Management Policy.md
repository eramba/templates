# End-Point Device Security

## Purpose
To ensure that end-point devices used to access organizational systems and data are managed and protected in a consistent and secure manner, reducing the risk of data loss, malware infection, and unauthorized access.

## Scope
This document applies to all end-point devices used to access organizational systems or data, including laptops, desktops, mobile devices, and other computing devices, whether owned by the organization or approved for organizational use.

## Responsibilities
- **Department Managers** are responsible for approving end-point device usage within their areas.
- **The Information Security function** is responsible for defining end-point security requirements and supporting their implementation, with the assistance of Department Managers.
- **IT / System Administrators** are responsible for managing and enforcing technical controls on organization-owned devices.
- **Users (employees and contractors)** are responsible for using end-point devices in accordance with this policy and protecting assigned devices.

## Policy Statements
- Only approved end-point devices may be used to access organizational systems or data.
- End-point devices must be managed and secured to reduce the risk of compromise.
- Security controls must be enforced consistently across end-point devices.
- Loss, theft, or compromise of end-point devices must be reported promptly.
- End-point devices must be backed up to support device recovery.

## Standards

### Device Ownership and Approval
- Organization-owned end-point devices must be issued through approved processes.
- Personally owned devices may only be used for organizational purposes if explicitly approved by the user’s department manager.
- Unapproved personal devices must not access organizational systems or data.

### Device Management
- End-point devices must be centrally managed where technically feasible.
- Security configurations must be enforced to prevent users from disabling required security controls.
- Devices must be configured according to approved baseline configurations.
- **Use of removable storage media (e.g. USB drives, external storage) is not permitted unless explicitly approved.**

### Authentication and Access
- Access to end-point devices must require user authentication.
- Authentication must be integrated with a central identity source where technically feasible.
- Users must not share device credentials.
- **User privileges on end-point devices must be assigned based on the user’s organizational role.**
- Elevated or administrative privileges must be restricted and granted only where justified.
- Automatic locking must be enabled when devices are unattended.

### Encryption
- Data stored on end-point devices must be encrypted using organization-approved encryption mechanisms.
- Encryption must be enabled prior to granting access to organizational data.

### Malware Protection
- End-point devices must have organization-approved anti-malware protection installed.
- Anti-malware software must be kept up to date.
- Malware protection must not be disabled by users.

### Patching and Updates
- Operating systems and applications must be kept up to date.
- Security updates must be applied automatically where technically feasible.
- Delays or exceptions to patching must follow standard organizational processes.

### Device Backup (System-Level)
- **Laptops must be backed up at least weekly** to support device recovery.
- Device backups must include system configuration and components required to restore the device.
- **Backup copies must be retained for a minimum of 30 days.**
- Device backup encryption, retention, and protection must follow the **Backup & Recovery Policy** and **Cryptography Policy**.
- These requirements apply to the **device system itself** and do not replace data backup requirements defined elsewhere.

### Network and Internet Use
- End-point devices must connect to organizational networks using approved methods.
- Remote access must use secure and approved mechanisms.
- Devices must not expose organizational services directly to the internet.

### Monitoring and Logging
- Security-relevant activity on end-point devices must be logged where technically feasible.
- End-point security events must be monitored to detect potential compromise.

### Device Loss and Disposal
- Loss or theft of end-point devices must be reported immediately.
- Data on lost or stolen devices must be protected or remotely wiped where supported.
- Devices must be securely wiped or disposed of when no longer required.

## Procedures

### End-Point Provisioning Procedure
1. Device requirements are identified and approved.
2. Devices are configured with approved security baselines.
3. Required security controls are enabled.
4. Devices are issued to users.

### End-Point Decommissioning Procedure
1. Devices are collected or remotely disabled.
2. Organizational data is securely removed.
3. Devices are wiped or disposed of according to organizational procedures.
