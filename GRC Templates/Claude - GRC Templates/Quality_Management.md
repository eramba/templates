# Quality Management

## Purpose
To ensure the organisation consistently delivers products and services that meet customer and regulatory requirements, and to drive continual improvement of quality management processes.

## Scope
All processes, products, services, and functions that affect the quality of outputs delivered to customers.

## Responsibilities

- **Senior Management**: Demonstrate leadership, establish quality policy and objectives, and ensure customer focus.
- **Quality Manager**: Maintain the quality management system, coordinate audits, and report on performance.
- **Process Owners**: Ensure their processes meet quality requirements and contribute to continual improvement.
- **All Personnel**: Understand their role in quality, follow defined processes, and report nonconformities.

## Policy Statements

### Customer Focus and Requirements

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Customer requirements must be determined, reviewed, and understood before committing to supply products or services.
- Customer satisfaction must be monitored and the results used to drive improvement.
- Statutory and regulatory requirements applicable to products and services must be identified and met.

**ISO 9001:2015 (5.1.2, 8.2.1, 8.2.2, 8.2.3.1, 8.2.3.2, 8.2.4)**
- Changes to customer requirements must be communicated to relevant personnel and documented.
- Risks to conformity of products and services must be addressed through the planning process.

### Process Control and Operational Planning

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Operational processes must be planned, implemented, and controlled to meet quality requirements.
- Documented information must be retained to demonstrate processes have been carried out as planned.
- Changes to processes must be controlled and reviewed for their impact on quality.
- Outputs not conforming to requirements must be identified, controlled, and addressed.

**ISO 9001:2015 (8.1, 8.5.1, 8.5.2, 8.5.3, 8.5.4, 8.5.5, 8.5.6)**
- Traceability must be maintained for outputs where it is a requirement.
- Property belonging to customers or external providers must be identified, protected, and safeguarded.

### Supplier and External Provider Management

**Applies to all frameworks**
- External providers must be evaluated and selected based on their ability to supply products and services meeting requirements.
- Requirements for externally provided products and services must be communicated clearly.
- Verification activities must be performed to confirm externally provided products and services meet requirements.

### Nonconformity and Corrective Action

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Nonconformities must be identified, documented, and controlled to prevent unintended use or delivery.
- Corrective actions must be implemented to eliminate the root cause of nonconformities.
- The effectiveness of corrective actions must be verified.
- Continual improvement of the quality management system must be pursued.

## Standards

*Standards define the specific technical and operational requirements that implement the policy statements above.*

## Procedures

### Customer Requirements Review

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Capture and document customer requirements prior to accepting an order or contract.
2. Review requirements for completeness, feasibility, and compliance with regulatory obligations.
3. Identify and resolve any differences between contract requirements and previously expressed requirements.
4. Obtain customer confirmation of requirements where applicable.
5. Communicate requirements to relevant internal functions.
6. Document the review outcome and retain records.

### Nonconformity Management

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Identify and log the nonconformity including description, location, and affected product or service.
2. Segregate or contain the nonconforming output to prevent unintended use or delivery.
3. Evaluate the nonconformity and determine disposition: rework, concession, or reject.
4. Conduct root cause analysis and define corrective actions.
5. Implement corrective actions and verify effectiveness.
6. Document and retain records of the nonconformity and actions taken.
