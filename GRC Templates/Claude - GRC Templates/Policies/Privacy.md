# Privacy

## Purpose
To ensure that personally identifiable information (PII) is processed lawfully, transparently, and only for defined and authorised purposes, and that individuals are informed of and can exercise their rights.

## Scope
All systems, processes, and personnel involved in the collection, storage, processing, or transmission of personally identifiable information.

## Responsibilities

- **Privacy Officer / DPO**: Define and oversee PII processing requirements, maintain records of processing activities, and handle individual rights requests.
- **System Owners**: Ensure systems processing PII comply with privacy requirements.
- **Legal / Compliance**: Identify applicable privacy laws and regulatory obligations.
- **All Staff**: Process PII only for authorised purposes and report suspected privacy incidents immediately.

## Policy Statements

### Legal Basis and Authority to Process

**Applies to all frameworks**
- PII must only be processed where a documented legal basis or organisational authority exists.
- The purposes for processing PII must be identified, documented, and communicated to individuals.
- Processing of PII must be restricted to purposes compatible with those originally identified.
- Changes to PII processing purposes must be reviewed and, where required, individuals must be re-notified or re-consented.

**NIST 800-53 Rev5 (PT-2, PT-3)**
- Processing of PII must be monitored for changes and corrective actions implemented where processing deviates from authorised purposes.

**CIS Controls v8.1 (3.1, 3.2)**

**SCF 2025 (PRI-01, PRI-02, PRI-03)**

**NIS2 Article 21 (Article 21(2) (a))**

**ISO 27701:2025 (A.3.3, A.3.4, A.3.5, A.2.2.2, A.2.2.3)**

### Consent

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Where consent is the legal basis for processing, mechanisms must be in place to obtain informed, freely given consent prior to collection.
- Consent mechanisms must be clear and easy to understand.
- Individuals must be able to withdraw consent at any time; withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent.
- Records of consent must be maintained.

**SCF 2025 (PRI-04, PRI-05)**

**ISO 27701:2025 (A.3.6, A.3.7, A.3.8, A.2.2.4, A.2.2.5)**

### Privacy Notice

**Applies to all frameworks**
- A privacy notice must be provided to individuals at or before the point of PII collection.
- Privacy notices must be written in plain language and identify: the legal basis for processing, the purposes, who receives the data, retention periods, and individual rights.
- Privacy notices must be reviewed and updated when processing activities change.
- Additional safeguards must be applied to specific categories of sensitive PII (e.g. health data, biometrics, financial data).

**SCF 2025 (PRI-06, PRI-07, PRI-08)**

**ISO 27701:2025 (A.3.9, A.3.10, A.2.2.6, A.2.2.7)**

### Data Minimisation and Retention

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Only PII necessary for the identified processing purpose must be collected.
- PII must not be retained longer than necessary for its processing purpose.
- Retention periods for PII must be documented and enforced.
- PII must be securely disposed of at the end of its retention period.

**CIS Controls v8.1 (3.3)**

**SCF 2025 (PRI-09, PRI-10, PRI-11)**

**ISO 27701:2025 (A.3.11, A.3.12, A.3.13, A.3.14, A.2.2.7)**

## Standards

*Standards define the specific technical and operational requirements that implement the policy statements above. Each standard section inherits the framework grouping of its parent policy statement section.*

## Procedures

### PII Processing Assessment

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Identify the PII to be processed and the system or process involved.
2. Document the legal basis or authority for processing.
3. Confirm the processing purpose is defined and recorded.
4. Assess whether the processing is proportionate and limited to what is necessary.
5. Record the assessment and obtain sign-off from the Privacy Officer.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (P3.1, P4.1, P4.2)**
6. Confirm personal information is collected consistent with stated objectives per P3.1.
7. Confirm use is limited to identified purposes per P4.1.
8. Confirm retention is consistent with objectives per P4.2.

### Privacy Notice Publication and Maintenance

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Draft or update the privacy notice to reflect current processing activities.
2. Review notice for clarity, completeness, and legal compliance.
3. Publish the notice at all relevant collection points.
4. Review the notice at least annually and upon any change to processing activities.
5. Document review date and approver.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (P1.1, P2.1)**
6. Confirm notice communicates purpose, choices, types of data, and rights per P1.1.
7. Confirm data subjects are informed of available choices per P2.1.

### Individual Rights Request Handling

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Receive and log the individual's request (access, rectification, erasure, objection, portability).
2. Verify the identity of the requester.
3. Locate all PII held relating to the individual across relevant systems.
4. Fulfil the request within the legally required timeframe.
5. Document the request, actions taken, and outcome.
6. Notify the individual of the outcome.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (P5.1, P5.2, P8.1)**
7. Confirm data subjects can access their personal information per P5.1.
8. Confirm corrections and amendments are processed per P5.2.
9. Confirm a process exists for receiving and resolving inquiries and complaints per P8.1.

### Data Subject Rights Request Fulfilment Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Review the data subject rights request log for the period.
2. Confirm each request was acknowledged and fulfilled within the required timeframe.
3. Confirm the organisation demonstrated ability to locate and act on PII.
4. Document any requests not fulfilled on time and the reason.
5. Identify systemic issues and raise corrective actions.

### PII Cross-Border Transfer Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Review the data transfer register for completeness.
2. Confirm legal mechanism for each transfer is current and valid.
3. Confirm data subjects are informed of transfers as required.
4. Document review completion and retain evidence.
