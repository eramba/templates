# Occupational Health Safety

## Purpose
To ensure the organisation provides a safe and healthy workplace by systematically identifying, assessing, and controlling occupational health and safety (OH&S) hazards and risks, and by meeting all applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

## Scope
All workers including employees, contractors, and visitors at all organisational locations and worksites.

## Responsibilities

- **Senior Management**: Demonstrate leadership, provide resources, and ensure integration of OH&S requirements into business processes.
- **OH&S Manager / Coordinator**: Maintain the OH&S management system, coordinate hazard identification, and report performance to management.
- **Line Managers / Supervisors**: Ensure OH&S controls are implemented within their areas and that workers are competent and informed.
- **All Workers**: Follow OH&S procedures, report hazards and incidents, and participate in consultation processes.

## Policy Statements

### Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Hazards must be proactively identified for all work activities, locations, and equipment.
- OH&S risks associated with identified hazards must be assessed and prioritised.
- Risk assessment results must be documented, reviewed, and used to determine controls.
- Hazard identification and risk assessment must be repeated periodically and when changes occur.

**ISO 45001:2018 (6.1.1, 6.1.2.1, 6.1.2.2, 6.1.2.3)**
- The hazard identification process must consider human factors, work organisation, and social factors.
- Legal and other requirements applicable to OH&S must be identified, documented, and kept current.

### OH&S Controls and Objectives

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Controls must be implemented to eliminate hazards and reduce OH&S risks, prioritising elimination over personal protective equipment.
- OH&S objectives must be established, documented, and monitored.
- Operational controls must be maintained and reviewed for effectiveness.

**ISO 45001:2018 (6.1.3, 6.1.4, 6.2.1, 6.2.2)**
- The hierarchy of controls must be applied: elimination, substitution, engineering controls, administrative controls, then PPE.
- Emergency preparedness and response plans must be established and tested.

### Worker Participation and Consultation

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Workers must be consulted and participate in the development, implementation, and review of OH&S policies and controls.
- Workers must be able to report hazards, near-misses, and incidents without fear of reprisal.
- OH&S information must be communicated to workers in a timely and understandable manner.

### Incident Management and Improvement

**Applies to all frameworks**
- All OH&S incidents, near-misses, and unsafe conditions must be reported, investigated, and documented.
- Root causes of incidents must be identified and corrective actions implemented to prevent recurrence.
- OH&S performance must be monitored and reviewed periodically by management.

**ISO 45001:2018 (8.2, 10.1, 10.2, 10.3)**
- Workers must be able to stop work and evacuate when they face imminent serious danger.

## Standards

*Standards define the specific technical and operational requirements that implement the policy statements above.*

## Procedures

### Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Define the scope of the hazard identification exercise (location, activity, or change).
2. Identify all hazards associated with the scope using walkthroughs, worker input, and incident history.
3. Assess the likelihood and severity of harm for each hazard.
4. Determine and implement controls using the hierarchy of controls.
5. Document results and assign owners for control implementation.
6. Review and update the risk register at defined intervals.

### OH&S Incident Reporting and Investigation

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Report the incident or near-miss to the line manager and OH&S coordinator immediately.
2. Provide first aid and emergency response as required.
3. Preserve the scene and collect evidence.
4. Conduct root cause analysis within defined timeframe.
5. Implement corrective actions and verify effectiveness.
6. Document the investigation and share learnings with relevant workers.

### OH&S Legal Compliance Evaluation Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Review the register of applicable OH&S legal requirements for currency.
2. Evaluate compliance status for each requirement.
3. Document any gaps and assign corrective actions.
4. Obtain sign-off from the OH&S manager.
5. Retain evaluation records.

### OH&S Performance Monitoring Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Compile OH&S performance data for the period: incidents, near-misses, hazard actions, objective status.
2. Analyse trends and compare against targets.
3. Identify adverse trends requiring intervention.
4. Document findings and present to management.
5. Retain report as evidence of monitoring.
