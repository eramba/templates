# Anti Bribery

## Purpose
To prevent bribery in all forms across the organisation's operations and business relationships, and to demonstrate compliance with applicable anti-bribery laws and regulations.

## Scope
All employees, officers, directors, contractors, agents, and business associates acting on behalf of the organisation, in all jurisdictions where the organisation operates.

## Responsibilities

- **Senior Management / Board**: Demonstrate commitment to anti-bribery, provide resources, and review programme effectiveness.
- **Compliance / Legal**: Maintain and update the anti-bribery programme, conduct risk assessments, and oversee due diligence.
- **Finance**: Implement financial controls to prevent and detect bribery including gifts, hospitality, and facilitation payments.
- **All Personnel**: Comply with this policy, refuse bribes, report concerns, and complete required training.

## Policy Statements

### Bribery Risk Assessment

**Applies to all frameworks**
- The organisation must conduct a bribery risk assessment covering all relevant operations, geographies, and business relationships.
- Bribery risks must be evaluated considering the likelihood and potential impact of bribery occurring.
- Risk assessment results must be used to design and prioritise anti-bribery controls.
- The bribery risk assessment must be reviewed periodically and upon significant changes.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 6.1, 37001:2025 - 6.2, 37001:2025 - 6.3)**
- The risk assessment must consider risks associated with business associates and third parties.

### Prohibitions and Controls

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Offering, promising, giving, accepting, or soliciting bribes in any form is strictly prohibited.
- Facilitation payments are prohibited regardless of local custom or practice.
- Gifts, hospitality, and expenses must be reasonable, proportionate, and accurately recorded.
- All transactions must be accurately recorded in the organisation's books and records.
- No undisclosed off-book accounts or funds may be maintained.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 8.1, 37001:2025 - 8.2, 37001:2025 - 8.3, 37001:2025 - 8.4, 37001:2025 - 8.5.1, 37001:2025 - 8.5.2)**
- Controls must be applied to high-risk transactions including procurement, licensing, permits, and public official interactions.
- Financial controls must be designed to detect unusual payments or transactions that may indicate bribery.

### Due Diligence on Business Associates

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Due diligence must be performed on business associates before engagement, proportionate to the assessed bribery risk.
- Anti-bribery commitments must be included in contracts with business associates.
- Business associate due diligence must be reviewed periodically and upon material changes.

### Reporting and Non-Retaliation

**Applies to all frameworks**
- All personnel must report suspected or actual bribery via the organisation's reporting channels.
- Retaliation against individuals who report bribery concerns in good faith is prohibited.
- Reported concerns must be investigated promptly and impartially.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 8.9, 37001:2025 - 8.10)**
- Whistleblowing channels must be accessible to both internal and external parties.

## Procedures

### Gifts and Hospitality Approval

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Assess whether the gift or hospitality is reasonable, proportionate, and not intended to influence a decision.
2. For items above the defined threshold, obtain prior approval from the line manager and compliance.
3. Record the item in the gifts and hospitality register including description, value, recipient, and business justification.
4. Decline or return gifts that cannot be approved.

### Business Associate Due Diligence

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Classify the business associate by bribery risk level based on geography, sector, and nature of engagement.
2. Conduct due diligence proportionate to the risk level (basic screening to enhanced investigation).
3. Include anti-bribery contractual obligations in the engagement agreement.
4. Record due diligence findings and approvals.
5. Review at defined intervals or upon material changes to the relationship.

### Bribery Concern Investigation

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Receive and log the reported concern.
2. Assign an investigator who is independent of the subject of the concern.
3. Conduct a confidential investigation collecting relevant evidence.
4. Conclude the investigation with documented findings.
5. Implement corrective actions and notify relevant parties as appropriate.
6. Report material findings to senior management and, where required, to authorities.

### Anti-Bribery Risk Assessment Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Review the bribery risk assessment against current operations, geographies, and business relationships.
2. Identify any new high-risk areas or changes since the last assessment.
3. Confirm controls are in place for all high-risk areas.
4. Update the assessment and obtain sign-off from the compliance function.
5. Retain review record with date and reviewer.

### Gifts and Hospitality Register Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Export the gifts and hospitality register for the review period.
2. Confirm all entries have required fields completed.
3. Identify items above threshold; confirm each has a documented approval.
4. Flag any entries without approval for investigation.
5. Document review completion and retain record.

### Anti-Bribery Due Diligence Review Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Review the business associate due diligence register for currency.
2. Identify high-risk associates not assessed within the last 12 months.
3. Commission reassessments for overdue high-risk associates.
4. Sample contracts to confirm anti-bribery clauses are present.
5. Document findings and update register.
