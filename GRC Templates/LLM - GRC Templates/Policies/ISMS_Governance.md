# ISMS Governance

## Purpose
To establish and maintain a management system for information security, occupational health and safety, AI, anti-bribery, and quality, demonstrating leadership commitment, defining scope, assigning roles and responsibilities, and ensuring adequate resources, communication, and documented information control.

## Scope
This policy applies to the entire organisation and all personnel involved in establishing, implementing, maintaining, or improving any management system within the organisation's defined scope.

## Responsibilities
- **Top Management** — demonstrate leadership and commitment; establish policies; assign roles and authorities; provide resources
- **Management System Owner** — maintain scope documentation; coordinate management reviews; ensure documented information is controlled
- **All Personnel** — understand their roles within the management system; comply with applicable requirements

## Policy Statements

### Context and Scope

**Applies to all frameworks**
- The organisation shall determine external and internal issues relevant to its purpose that may affect its ability to achieve the intended outcomes of its management systems.
- The organisation shall determine interested parties relevant to each management system and their requirements.
- The organisation shall establish, document, and maintain the scope of each management system.

**ISO 27001:2022 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
- The organisation shall determine interested parties relevant to the ISMS and their relevant requirements.
- The scope of the ISMS shall be defined in terms of boundaries and applicability, considering internal and external issues and interested party requirements.
- The ISMS shall be established, implemented, maintained, and continually improved in accordance with ISO 27001.

**ISO 45001:2018 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
- The organisation shall determine workers and other interested parties relevant to the OH&S management system and their needs and expectations.
- The scope of the OH&S management system shall be determined considering the nature of the organisation's activities, legal requirements, and obligations.
- The OH&S management system shall be established, implemented, maintained, and continually improved.

**ISO 42001:2023 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
- The organisation shall determine interested parties relevant to the AI management system and their relevant requirements.
- The scope of the AI management system shall be established considering AI systems developed, provided, or used.
- The AI management system shall be established, implemented, maintained, continually improved, and documented.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 4.2, 37001:2025 - 4.3, 37001:2025 - 4.4)**
- The organisation shall determine interested parties relevant to the ABMS and their relevant requirements.
- The scope of the ABMS shall be defined considering the nature, scale, and locations of the organisation's activities and the bribery risks it faces.
- The ABMS shall be established, implemented, maintained, and continually improved.

**ISO 9001:2015 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4.1, 4.4.2)**
- The organisation shall determine interested parties relevant to the QMS and their relevant requirements.
- The scope of the QMS shall be determined considering external and internal issues and the requirements of relevant interested parties.
- The QMS shall be established, implemented, maintained, and continually improved including the processes needed and their interactions.

**ISO 27701:2025 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
- Determine interested parties relevant to the PIMS and their relevant requirements per 4.2.
- Determine the scope of the PIMS considering the organisation's processing of PII per 4.3.
- Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve a PIMS per 4.4.

### Leadership and Commitment

**Applies to all frameworks**
- Top management shall demonstrate visible leadership and commitment to each management system by taking accountability for its effectiveness, establishing policies, ensuring integration into business processes, promoting continual improvement, and supporting other management roles.

**ISO 27001:2022 (5.1, 5.2, 5.3)**
- Top management shall ensure the information security policy and objectives are established and aligned with the organisation's strategic direction.
- Top management shall establish an information security policy that includes a commitment to satisfy applicable requirements and to continual improvement.
- Top management shall ensure that responsibilities and authorities for roles relevant to information security are assigned and communicated.

**ISO 45001:2018 (5.1, 5.2, 5.3)**
- Top management shall demonstrate leadership by taking overall responsibility for the prevention of work-related injury and ill health and provision of safe and healthy workplaces.
- Top management shall establish, implement, and maintain an OH&S policy that includes commitments to provide safe and healthy working conditions.
- Top management shall ensure that responsibilities and authorities for relevant roles are assigned and communicated.

**ISO 42001:2023 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
- Top management shall demonstrate leadership with respect to the AI management system by ensuring AI policy and objectives are established and aligned with the organisation's strategic direction.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 5.1.1, 37001:2025 - 5.1.2, 37001:2025 - 5.1.3, 37001:2025 - 5.2, 37001:2025 - 5.3.1, 37001:2025 - 5.3.2, 37001:2025 - 5.3.3)**
- Where the organisation has a separate governing body, that body shall demonstrate leadership and commitment with respect to the ABMS.
- Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment by ensuring the ABMS achieves its intended outcomes and is continually improved.
- The organisation shall develop, maintain, and promote an anti-bribery culture at all levels.
- Top management shall establish an anti-bribery policy that prohibits bribery and requires compliance with applicable anti-bribery laws.
- Top management shall have overall responsibility for the implementation of and compliance with the ABMS.
- An anti-bribery function shall be appointed with responsibility and authority for overseeing the ABMS.
- Where top management delegates authority for decisions involving bribery risk, the organisation shall establish appropriate controls over delegated decision-making.

**ISO 9001:2015 (5.1.1, 5.2.1, 5.2.2, 5.3)**
- Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment with respect to the QMS by taking accountability for the effectiveness of the QMS.
- Top management shall establish, implement, and maintain a quality policy appropriate to the purpose and context of the organisation.
- The quality policy shall be available as documented information, communicated, understood, and applied within the organisation.
- Top management shall ensure that responsibilities and authorities for relevant roles are assigned, communicated, and understood.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (CC1.2, CC1.3, CC2.3, CC5.1, CC5.2)**
- The entity shall select and develop control activities that contribute to the mitigation of risks to the achievement of objectives.
- The entity shall select and develop general control activities over technology to support the achievement of objectives.

**ISO 27002:2022 (5.4)**
- Management shall require all personnel to apply information security in accordance with the established information security policy, topic-specific policies, and procedures per 5.4.

**ISO 27701:2025 (5.1, 5.2, 5.3)**
- Top management shall demonstrate leadership with respect to the PIMS by ensuring privacy policy and objectives are established per 5.1.
- Top management shall establish a privacy policy including a commitment to meet applicable requirements and to continual improvement per 5.2.
- Top management shall ensure responsibilities and authorities for relevant PIMS roles are assigned and communicated per 5.3.

**PCI DSS v4.0.1 (12.1.1, 12.1.2, 12.1.3, 12.1.4, 12.5.1)**
- Establish, publish, maintain, and disseminate an overall information security policy per 12.1.1.
- Review the information security policy at least once every 12 months and update as needed per 12.1.2.
- Clearly define information security roles and responsibilities for all personnel in the security policy per 12.1.3.
- Assign responsibility for information security to a CISO or other information security knowledgeable member of executive management per 12.1.4.

### Resources

**Applies to all frameworks**
- The organisation shall determine and provide the resources needed for the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of each management system.
- Resources include human resources, infrastructure, environment, monitoring and measurement resources, and organisational knowledge.

**ISO 27001:2022 (7.1)**
- Resources needed for the ISMS shall be determined and provided including personnel, technology, and financial resources.

**ISO 45001:2018 (7.1)**
- Resources needed for the OH&S management system shall be determined and provided.

**ISO 42001:2023 (7.1)**
- Resources needed for the AI management system shall be determined and provided.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 7.1)**
- Resources needed for the ABMS shall be determined and provided, proportionate to the organisation's size and the bribery risks it faces.

**ISO 9001:2015 (7.1.4, 7.1.5.1, 7.1.5.2, 7.1.6)**
- The organisation shall determine, provide, and maintain the infrastructure and environment necessary for the operation of its processes.
- The organisation shall determine and provide resources needed to ensure valid and reliable results when monitoring or measuring conformity.
- The organisation shall determine the knowledge necessary for the operation of its processes and to achieve conformity of products and services.

**ISO 27701:2025 (7.1)**
- Determine and provide resources needed for the PIMS per 7.1.
- Ensure persons doing work that affects privacy performance are competent per 7.2.
- Ensure persons are aware of the privacy policy and their contribution to PIMS effectiveness per 7.3.

### Communication

**Applies to all frameworks**
- The organisation shall determine the need for internal and external communications relevant to each management system, including what, when, with whom, and how to communicate.

**ISO 27001:2022 (7.4)**
- The organisation shall determine internal and external communications relevant to the ISMS including what to communicate, when, with whom, and how.

**ISO 42001:2023 (7.4)**
- The organisation shall determine internal and external communications relevant to the AI management system.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 7.4.1, 37001:2025 - 7.4.2)**
- The organisation shall determine internal and external communications relevant to the ABMS.
- The anti-bribery policy shall be made available in appropriate languages to all personnel and business associates where appropriate.

**ISO 9001:2015 (7.4)**
- The organisation shall determine the internal and external communications relevant to the QMS.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (CC2.1, CC2.2, CC2.3)**
- The entity shall obtain or generate and use relevant, quality information to support the functioning of internal control.
- The entity shall internally communicate information including objectives and responsibilities for internal control necessary to support the functioning of internal control.
- The entity shall communicate with external parties regarding matters affecting the functioning of internal control.

**ISO 27701:2025 (7.4)**
- Determine internal and external communications relevant to the PIMS per 7.4.

**ISO 45001:2018 (7.4.1, 7.4.2, 7.4.3)**
- Establish, implement, and maintain processes for internal and external communications relevant to the OH&S management system per 7.4.1.
- Internally communicate information relevant to the OH&S management system among various levels and functions of the organisation per 7.4.2.
- Externally communicate information relevant to the OH&S management system as established by the organisation's communication process per 7.4.3.

### Documented Information

**Applies to all frameworks**
- The organisation shall create, update, and control documented information required by each management system and as determined necessary for the effectiveness of the management system.
- Documented information shall be available, suitable for use, and adequately protected.

**ISO 27001:2022 (7.5, 7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3)**
- The ISMS documented information shall include documented information required by ISO 27001 and determined by the organisation as necessary for the effectiveness of the ISMS.
- When creating and updating documented information the organisation shall ensure appropriate identification, format, review, and approval.
- Documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available, suitable for use, and adequately protected against loss of confidentiality, improper use, or loss of integrity.

**ISO 45001:2018 (7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3)**
- The OH&S management system documented information shall include documents required by ISO 45001 and documents determined necessary for effectiveness.
- When creating and updating OH&S documented information the organisation shall ensure appropriate identification and description, format, and review and approval.
- OH&S documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available, suitable for use, and adequately protected.

**ISO 42001:2023 (7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3)**
- The AI management system documented information shall include documents required by ISO 42001 and documents determined necessary for effectiveness.
- When creating and updating AI documented information the organisation shall ensure appropriate identification, format, and review.
- AI documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available, suitable for use, and adequately protected.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 7.5.1, 37001:2025 - 7.5.2, 37001:2025 - 7.5.3)**
- The ABMS documented information shall include documents required by ISO 37001 and documents determined necessary for effectiveness.
- When creating and updating ABMS documented information the organisation shall ensure appropriate identification, format, and review.
- ABMS documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available and adequately protected.

**ISO 9001:2015 (7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3.1, 7.5.3.2)**
- The QMS documented information shall include documents required by ISO 9001 and documents determined necessary for effectiveness.
- When creating and updating QMS documented information the organisation shall ensure appropriate identification, format, and review and approval.
- QMS documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available, suitable for use, and adequately protected.
- The organisation shall address distribution, access, retrieval, use, storage, preservation, version control, retention, and disposition of documented information.

**ISO 27002:2022 (5.37)**
- Operating procedures for information processing facilities shall be documented and made available to personnel who need them per 5.37.

**ISO 27701:2025 (7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3)**
- PIMS documented information shall include documents required by ISO 27701 and documents determined necessary for effectiveness per 7.5.1.
- When creating and updating PIMS documented information ensure appropriate identification, format, and review per 7.5.2.
- PIMS documented information shall be controlled to ensure it is available, suitable for use, and adequately protected per 7.5.3.

**PCI DSS v4.0.1 (1.1.2, 2.1.1, 3.1.2, 5.1.1, 5.1.2, 6.1.1, 6.1.2, 7.1.1, 7.1.2, 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 10.1.1, 10.1.2, 11.1.1, 11.1.2)**
- Document, keep up to date, and communicate all security policies and operational procedures for each PCI DSS requirement area, with roles and responsibilities assigned and understood per 1.1.2, 2.1.1, 3.1.2, 4.1.1, 4.1.2, 5.1.1, 5.1.2, 6.1.1, 6.1.2, 7.1.1, 7.1.2, 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 10.1.1, 10.1.2, 11.1.1, 11.1.2.

## Standards

- Management system scope documents shall be reviewed annually or when significant organisational changes occur.
- The information security policy, OH&S policy, AI policy, anti-bribery policy, and quality policy shall be approved by top management, communicated to all personnel, and reviewed at minimum annually.
- Role assignments and authorities for each management system shall be documented and kept up to date.
- Documented information shall be version-controlled, stored in an accessible location, and reviewed before publication.

## Procedures

### Management System Scope Review

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Confirm the scope document for each management system is current.
2. Review internal and external issues that may affect the scope.
3. Confirm interested parties and their requirements have been considered.
4. Update scope documentation if any changes apply.
5. Obtain top management approval for scope changes.
6. Communicate scope changes to relevant personnel.

**ISO 27001:2022 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4)**
7. Confirm the ISMS scope addresses the boundaries of information assets, systems, and locations covered.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 4.2, 37001:2025 - 4.3, 37001:2025 - 4.4)**
7. Confirm the ABMS scope reflects the organisation's activities, locations, and bribery risk profile.

**ISO 9001:2015 (4.2, 4.3, 4.4.1, 4.4.2)**
7. Confirm the QMS scope identifies the products and services covered and any exclusions with justification.

**PCI DSS v4.0.1 (12.5.2)**
- Document and confirm PCI DSS scope at least once every 12 months and upon significant change to the in-scope environment per 12.5.2.

### Leadership Commitment Review

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Confirm top management has approved current versions of all management system policies.
2. Confirm roles and responsibilities for each management system are assigned to named individuals.
3. Confirm resources allocated to each management system are adequate.
4. Document evidence of top management engagement in management reviews.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 5.1.1, 37001:2025 - 5.1.2, 37001:2025 - 5.3.2)**
5. Confirm the anti-bribery function role is assigned to a person with appropriate seniority and access to top management.
6. Confirm delegated decision-making controls are in place for positions with bribery risk exposure.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (CC5.1, CC5.2)**
5. Confirm control activities address risks identified in the risk assessment.
6. Confirm general controls over technology are operating effectively.

### Documented Information Control Procedure

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Confirm all required documented information exists and is current.
2. Confirm documents are identified with title, version, date, and author.
3. Confirm documents are stored in the approved location and accessible to relevant personnel.
4. Confirm obsolete versions are prevented from unintended use.
5. Confirm external documents of external origin are identified and controlled.
6. Review document retention schedule against legal and regulatory requirements.

**ISO 27001:2022 (7.5, 7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3)**
7. Confirm ISMS documented information is protected from loss of confidentiality and integrity.

**ISO 9001:2015 (7.5.1, 7.5.2, 7.5.3.1, 7.5.3.2)**
7. Confirm QMS documented information distribution, access, retrieval, and disposition controls are applied.

### Internal Communication Review

**Applies to all frameworks**
1. Confirm communication channels for management system information are defined and operating.
2. Confirm personnel are informed of their responsibilities and objectives.
3. Confirm external communication requirements are identified and met.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 7.4.1, 37001:2025 - 7.4.2)**
4. Confirm the anti-bribery policy is available in appropriate languages to all personnel and relevant business associates.

**SOC 2 (TSP 2017) (CC2.1, CC2.2, CC2.3)**
4. Confirm quality information is generated and used to support internal control functioning.
5. Confirm internal control information is communicated to support personnel in their responsibilities.
6. Confirm external communications on internal control matters are managed appropriately.

### Privacy Objectives and Operational Planning

**ISO 27701:2025 (6.1, 6.1.1, 6.2, 6.3, 8.1, 10.1, 10.2)**
- The organisation shall consider issues and requirements when planning for the PIMS and determine risks and opportunities that need to be addressed per 6.1 and 6.1.1.
- Privacy objectives shall be established at relevant functions and levels, be measurable, monitored, communicated, and updated as appropriate per 6.2.
- When the organisation determines the need for changes to the PIMS, the changes shall be carried out in a planned manner per 6.3.
- Processes needed to meet requirements and implement risk treatment actions shall be planned, implemented, and controlled per 8.1.
- The organisation shall continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the PIMS per 10.1.
- When a PIMS nonconformity occurs, the organisation shall react, evaluate the need for corrective action, implement actions, and verify effectiveness per 10.2.

**ISO 42001:2023 (10.1, 10.2)**
- Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the AI management system per 10.1.
- When an AI management system nonconformity occurs, react, evaluate the need for corrective action, implement, and verify effectiveness per 10.2.

**ISO 37001:2025 (37001:2025 - 10.1, 37001:2025 - 10.2)**
- Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ABMS per 10.1.
- When an ABMS nonconformity occurs, react, evaluate the need for corrective action, implement, and verify effectiveness per 10.2.

**ISO 9001:2015 (10.1, 10.3)**
- Determine and select opportunities for improvement and implement actions to meet customer requirements and enhance customer satisfaction per 10.1.
- Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the QMS per 10.3.

### Privacy Objectives and Operational Planning Procedure

**ISO 27701:2025 (6.1, 6.1.1, 6.2, 6.3, 8.1, 10.1, 10.2)**
1. Confirm privacy objectives are documented, measurable, and aligned with the privacy policy.
2. Review achievement of privacy objectives for the current period.
3. Set or update privacy objectives for the next period with assigned responsibilities and timelines.
4. Confirm planned changes to the PIMS are being carried out in a controlled manner.
5. Confirm processes needed to meet privacy requirements are planned and operating.
6. React to any PIMS nonconformities — evaluate need for corrective action, implement, and verify effectiveness.
7. Confirm the PIMS is being continually improved based on audit results, management review outputs, and corrective actions.

**NIST 800-53 Rev5 (PM-1, PM-2, PM-3, PM-29, SA-2)**
- Develop and maintain information security program plan per PM-1.
- Appoint senior agency information security officer per PM-2.
- Allocate resources required to protect systems per PM-3.
- Assign senior leadership roles for risk management per PM-29.
- Allocate resources required to protect systems per SA-2.
